Article Content

A complete side-scrolling action guide including boss strategies

Shin Nekketsu Kōha: Kunio‑tachi no Banka – SNES (新・熱血硬派くにおたちの挽歌)

April 29, 1994, developed by Almanic Corporation under the Technōs Japan brand and published by Technōs Japan, Shin Nekketsu Kōha: Kunio‑tachi no Banka is a Super Famicom–exclusive belt‑scrolling action game. Unlike prior entries’ comic style, it introduces realistic character proportions and dramatic dialogue.

1. Basic Overview & Release Information

  • Title: Shin Nekketsu Kōha: Kunio‑tachi no Banka
  • Platform: Super Famicom (SFC)
  • Genre: Beat ’em up
  • Release Date: April 29, 1994 (Japan)
  • Developer: Almanic Corporation
  • Publisher: Technōs Japan

2. Development & Production Background

Directed by series creator Yoshihisa Kishimoto, with Noriyuki Tomiyama (producer), Michiya Hirasawa (sound programmer), Kazunaka Yamane (composer), and Hiro Yokokura (scenario writer) as key staff. Development shifted from in‑house Technōs Japan to Almanic, influencing the engine and level design later seen in Super Mad Champ. A planned PlayStation sequel was never released.

3. Game Systems & Features

  • Playable Characters: Kunio, Riki, unlockable Misako & Kyōko (4 total)
  • Party System: Up to 2 players onscreen; shared health gauge instead of lives; character swapping
  • Combat Actions: Punch, kick, jump, block, back‑attack; limited moves in prison uniform; grapples & specials in school uniform
  • Bike Stage: Kunio (1P) and Riki (2P) ride motorcycles in a unique but notoriously difficult segment
  • Story Emphasis: Original dialogue cutscenes before/after stages for a cinematic feel

4. Detailed Level Structure & Strategy

Note: Play on Normal mode or higher to experience the full story. Easy mode ends the game prematurely.

4.0 Synopsis

Late at night in Tokyo, a hit‑and‑run involving high school students on motorcycles occurs. Witnesses identify Kunio (Nekketsu High) and Riki (Hanazono High). Both are imprisoned in Nerima Juvenile Detention Center, wearing prison uniforms that limit them to basic punches and kicks. Next morning, during a visit by Hiroshi, they learn rumors of simultaneous sightings in town. Doubting the charges, they join cellmate Gouji to plan an escape. That night, they raid the guard post, break out, and set off to clear their names.

4.1 Detention Center

  • Content:
    Kunio and Riki are accused of the hit‑and‑run and sent to Nerima Juvenile Detention Center. In cell 218, they clash with boss‑level inmate Gouji to prove their strength. During Hiroshi’s visit, they hear of rumors placing them near Nekketsu High.
    • 3:26 Tutorial‑style stage vs. Gouji’s group. Master distance control and vertical movement.
      • Stomping a downed opponent is highly effective.
    • 9:10 Combat vs. guards during breakout. Lighting is purely atmospheric.
    • 10:06 Hiroshi admits he anticipated their escape and prepared in advance.
    • 10:56 Boss Battle: Warden Takayama
      • Early‑stage fight with high player health—straightforward.

4.2 Nekketsu High School

  • Content:
    12:19 Escape the detention center and infiltrate Nekketsu High via a broken fence. Near the pool, meet Misako, who warns of a white‑uniformed impostor of Kunio.

    • 14:31 Ascend to the 3rd floor to confront Daiki. Enemies are manageable, but health management is crucial (no mid‑stage healing).
    • 17:11 Boss Battle: Daiki
      • Daiki is stronger than regular foes. He gains invincibility frames when rising—manage spacing and eliminate his henchmen quickly.

  • Misako joins your team, and with Mamoru’s help you evade the authorities and proceed to the next location.

4.3 Hanazono High School

  • Content:
    23:02 Hanazono High lies in ruins. During Riki’s absence, Ryūta seized control of the school and forced students to labor at a nearby amusement park. Upon learning this, Kunio and Riki burst into the school to stop him.
    • 24:12 As at Nekketsu High, you must climb to the 3rd floor. Differences here:
      • Enemies are stronger—more aggressive and higher health.
      • 25:01 The floor collapses underfoot due to the ruin. Without a well‑timed jump you’ll take damage; 25:53 shows a successful evade.
    • 27:38 Boss Fight: Ryūta
      • 29:37 Defeat Daiki’s henchmen first—they’re tough too.
      • Ryūta gains invincibility frames when rising, so manage your spacing.
      • 28:40, 29:53 The school continues to crumble eight times total—jump each time to avoid damage (it’s minor and can be ignored).
      • 31:25 Kyōko joins your party.
      • 31:49 You fight enemies again as you depart for the local amusement park.

4.4 Amusement Park

  • Content:
    33:09 Upon entering the park, Ryūsaku breathes a sigh of relief at seeing Riki and reports that Shinji has been in contact with Ken and was last seen entering the Ferris wheel area.
    • 34:27 The Ferris wheel zone is notoriously frustrating: sightlines are blocked, the repeated climbing is tedious, enemies are scattered, and two‑player co‑op splits your view, making navigation and combat unduly fatiguing.
    • 37:46 Next, you move on to the Jet Coaster area to continue the fight.
    • 38:51 Riding the coaster restores health; you remain aboard for about two minutes until 41:29.
    • 41:51 Boss Fight: Shinji
      • Shinji is a formidable opponent:
        • 42:40 He uses a punch combo dealing roughly one‑third of your health.
        • 43:09 He also employs a roundhouse kick.

4.5 Highway Travel 1

  • Content:
    44:48 Defeated in battle, Shinji flees to Pier 13. Kunio’s group speeds down the highway on motorcycles.
    • Slow down for curves—too much speed makes steering impossible.
    • 45:30 A precisely timed kick can knock bikers off their mounts, though it often requires luck.
    • 45:43 If you have enough health, ramming enemies into walls is more efficient—just avoid crashing yourself.

4.6 Pier 13 Harbor

  • Content:
    47:28 After battling karate fighters in the warehouse area, you use hooks in two adjoining rooms to close the distance and face Shinji again.
    • 47:42 You first fight bikers at the docks; no healing is available until the stage is cleared, so manage your health.
    • 48:53 Next is the warehouse interior:
      • 49:15 You can jump and grab ceiling hooks—though it offers no special advantage.
    • 50:40 A 2D stage with no vertical axis—you must climb hanging crates.
      • 50:57 Bikers continually interfere, making the climb more tedious.
    • 52:56 Another horizontal‑only stage; maintain spacing during combat.
    • 54:07 Boss Fight: Shinji
      • Quickly eliminate his allies—then Shinji is not especially difficult.
      • After defeat, Shinji claims ignorance about Ken, but Misako realizes he’s been acting under Ken’s orders. He warns you not to meddle, or you’ll face dire consequences.
    • 55:45 Outside the warehouse, Warden Takayama asks Kunio to arrest Ken and Sabu, revealing the shocking truth that Ken may be Kunio’s long‑lost brother. Raised in an orphanage and adopted by Sabu two years ago, Ken swore revenge against Kunio. He was last seen at a Roppongi disco.

4.7 Highway Travel 2

  • Content:
    58:00 You ride the highway to Roppongi to track down Ken. No special differences from other highway stages—drive safely.

4.8 Roppongi Disco Club

  • Content:
    59:53 Arriving at the disco to find Ken, you encounter Lisa and Joe.
    • 59:53 Boss Fight: Lisa & Joe
      • Joe has invincibility frames when rising—keep your distance.
      • Lisa is weaker but dangerous in tandem; defeat them quickly.
    • 1:02:10 Boss Fight: Kinji & Lisa
      • Kinji uses powerful uppercut punches and double kicks—these deal massive damage.
      • Kinji guards frequently—be patient.
      • Lisa remains a minor threat but fight cautiously.
    • Defeated, Kinji admits Ken is in the Pachinko Hall office in Shinjuku.

4.9 Highway Travel 3

  • Content:
    1:05:19 You ride the highway to Shinjuku to confront Ken. No special differences from other highway segments—stay alert.

4.10 Shinjuku Pachinko Office

  • Content:
    1:06:58 Upon arriving at the Pachinko office, yakuza immediately greet Kunio’s group.
    • 1:07:14 Stomping and mount attacks are highly effective in this game.
    • 1:07:56 Boss Fight: Misuzu
      • Misuzu is a recurring boss in the Kunio series, known for her great strength, and appears here as a formidable opponent.
      • Although slow, she has moves that rapidly close distance, making her very threatening.
      • 1:08:53 Without any telegraphing, she hits you with her signature ‘Shoulder Smash - 鐵山靠’, which shaves off 25% of your HP.
    • 1:10:26 Quickly dispatch the two yakuza.
    • 1:10:56 Kunio’s group meets Ken and Sabu in the office.
      • As tensions peak, Sabu suddenly draws an automatic pistol and shoots Misako and Kyōko. Shocked, Kunio, Riki, and Ken watch in horror as Sabu drags Ken away. Kunio and Riki rush to the fallen Misako and Kyōko, only to find them unconscious.
      • 1:13:15 Immediately afterward, Takayama and Mamoru meet them outside the Pachinko hall. Takayama reports that Sabu and Ken have fled to Sabu’s villa in Izu, and arranges police escort for Kunio and Riki. He also orders Mamoru to call an ambulance for Misako and Kyōko.
    • From this point on, Misako and Kyōko can no longer be selected as playable characters.

4.11 Highway Travel 4

  • Content:
    1:14:06 Kunio and Riki set out for Izu to pursue Sabu’s villa.
    • The motorcycle ride proceeds automatically.

4.12 Izu, Sabu’s Villa

  • Content:
    1:14:35 Kunio and Riki meet Takayama near a wooden bridge. He informs them their innocence in the hit‑and‑run has been confirmed and no charges remain.
    • 1:15:02 They fight the students guarding the villa.
    • 1:15:44 As they cross, Ken appears with a torch, sets the bridge ablaze, and Kunio’s party plunges into the river.
    • 1:16:13 They survive on floating debris and battle more guards.
    • 1:18:05 They enter a cave converted like a factory and escape via moving platforms.
    • 1:18:43 Reaching the villa, they fight bodyguards at the entrance.
    • 1:19:30 Confronting Sabu triggers a trap, dropping them into the catacombs.
    • 1:19:48 A burly boxer awaits in the catacombs.
      • 1:19:55 The floor collapses if you stand too long—time your attacks to drop him.
    • 1:20:24 They fight female guards styled like Lisa; stronger than Lisa, so manage health carefully.
    • 1:23:16 Boss Fight: Misuzu
      • This is a rematch with Misuzu from Shinjuku. She claims she guards Sabu because he’d erase her Pachinko debts.
      • 1:25:08 She warns that the villa is rigged with a self‑destruct device.
      • Winning this fight fully restores your health.
    • 1:25:42 They battle more bodyguards.
    • 1:26:14 Boss Fight: Ken
      • Ken’s stats match Kunio’s except he lacks “Rapid Punch.”
      • He has the highest health in the game and attacks aggressively—on par with Misuzu’s difficulty.
      • 1:28:04 After his defeat, Sabu calls him worthless and shoots him.
        • Ken reveals he is not Kunio’s real brother and was adopted by Sabu for revenge.
        • He warns of the activated self‑destruct device and urges them to flee.
        • Before dying, he confesses he wanted to call Kunio “brother” at least once.
    • 1:29:17 Boss Fight: Sabu
      • In the burning room, Sabu rages that without Kunio he would have ruled Shinjuku and Roppongi, vowing to destroy him.
      • He carries a pistol that deals about 4/5 of your health in one shot—nearly instant death.
      • This stage is strictly 2D (no vertical movement), so stay close to Sabu to avoid his shots.

4.13 Ending

Kunio and Riki watch Sabu’s villa explode and collapse. Takayama arrives, reporting the police have arrested all of Sabu’s henchmen, and that Misako and Kyōko will survive. The opening sequence plays again, but this time Kunio and Riki stop their bike, and Kunio warns a schoolgirl to be more careful crossing the street next time.

5. Reputation & Reviews

At launch, Famitsu rated it 24/40 (60%), eliciting mixed opinions.
Western outlet Super Console gave 85/100, praising its originality and dramatic presentation, though the bike stage’s difficulty drew criticism.
MobyGames calls it “on par with SNES beat ’em up classics,” averaging 66%.
The 2022 remaster River City Girls Zero holds a Metascore 68 (Mixed/Average), lauded as a faithful reinterpretation.

6. Additional Info & Sequels

  • 2019: WayForward released River City Girls, a full 2D spin‑off continuing Misako & Kyōko’s story.
  • Feb 14, 2022: River City Girls Zero, a global localized remaster, launched for Switch, PS4/PS5, Windows, Xbox, etc.

7. Conclusion & Summary

Shin Nekketsu Kōha: Kunio‑tachi no Banka broke new ground in 1994 with its cinematic storytelling and party system. Though reviews were mixed initially, its remaster and spin‑offs have cemented its legacy as a cornerstone of the Kunio series.
